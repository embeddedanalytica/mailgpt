Here’s a simple email-coaching decision tree that works across mixed-sport athletes. It’s designed to do two things fast:
	1.	classify “where they stand” (level + focus + constraints), then
	2.	decide “what to do next” (plan type + next email + today’s action).

Decision tree overview (3 layers)

Layer 1 — Intake classification (do once, then update as needed)

Q1. Primary goal timeframe?
	•	A: General health / consistency (no date)
	•	B: Event goal (date within 8–16 weeks)
	•	C: Performance goal (date >16 weeks or ongoing)

Q2. Training identity (what are they really)?
	•	A: Casual multi-activity (“I do a bit of everything”)
	•	B: Single-discipline focused (mostly run OR mostly bike OR mostly swim)
	•	C: Hybrid seasonal (main sport shifts by season; cross-train is real)

Q3. Experience level in their main discipline
	•	A: New (0–6 months consistent)
	•	B: Intermediate (6–24 months consistent)
	•	C: Advanced amateur (2+ years consistent + structured training history)

Q4. Availability + constraints
	•	Time: 2–3h/wk | 4–6h/wk | 7–10h/wk | 10h+/wk
	•	Injury risk: none | recurring niggles | current pain
	•	Logistics: equipment access (gym/pool/bike/trainer), schedule variability (low/med/high)

👉 Output of Layer 1: a simple label like
“B / C / Intermediate / 4–6h / recurring niggles / high variability”
(Event goal + seasonal hybrid + intermediate + moderate time + some risk + variable schedule)

⸻

Layer 2 — Weekly plan selection (the core branch)

Q5. Do they have one “main sport” right now?
	•	Yes → build a Main Sport Plan
	•	No → build a General Fitness Plan

If General Fitness Plan
Pick one based on time:
	•	2–3h/wk: 3 sessions total
	•	1 “engine” (easy aerobic), 1 strength/mobility, 1 fun/variety
	•	4–6h/wk: 4 sessions
	•	2 aerobic, 1 strength, 1 optional intensity OR skills
	•	7h+/wk: 5–6 sessions
	•	3 aerobic, 1 intensity, 1 strength, 1 optional (skills or recovery)

Rules:
	•	Keep intensity low unless they explicitly want performance.
	•	Rotate modalities to reduce injury (run/bike/swim/gym).

If Main Sport Plan
Q6. What phase are we in?
	•	Base (no event soon / rebuilding consistency)
	•	Build (event 6–12 weeks away OR chasing performance)
	•	Peak/Taper (event 0–3 weeks away)
	•	Return-to-training (coming back from injury/illness or long break)

Q7. Risk flag?
	•	Red: pain during activity, sharp pain, worsening week-to-week → reduce load + swap to low impact + referral language
	•	Yellow: recurring niggles, heavy fatigue, sleep/stress high → keep volume, reduce intensity
	•	Green: normal fatigue only → proceed

Then choose weekly “skeleton” (works for run/bike/swim):

Time 2–3h/wk (3 sessions)
	•	1 long/easy session (main sport)
	•	1 short quality OR hills/tempo (only if Green)
	•	1 strength/mobility OR cross-train easy

Time 4–6h/wk (4 sessions)
	•	2 easy aerobic (main sport)
	•	1 quality (intervals/tempo/threshold depending on level)
	•	1 strength OR cross-train

Time 7–10h/wk (5–6 sessions)
	•	3 easy aerobic
	•	1 long
	•	1 quality
	•	1 strength or skills

Time 10h+/wk
	•	Add a second quality day only for Intermediate/Advanced + Green + stable schedule

⸻

Layer 3 — Session-level decision (what to tell them today)

Every email check-in gets routed by 4 quick signals:

Signal 1: Pain
	•	Pain ≥4/10 or changes gait/form → Stop intensity, swap to easy cross-train, and adjust next 3–7 days
	•	Pain 1–3/10 stable → keep easy, no intensity, monitor

Signal 2: Fatigue/energy
	•	Energy ≤4/10 → convert to “minimum effective dose” session
	•	Energy 5–7/10 → do planned but conservative
	•	Energy 8–10/10 → do planned or upgrade slightly (only if Green)

Signal 3: Missed sessions
	•	Missed 1 session → don’t “make up” intensity; resume plan
	•	Missed 2+ sessions → rebuild week: easy volume first, delay intensity

Signal 4: Schedule reality
	•	If week is chaotic → prioritize the “Big 2”
	1.	one long/easy aerobic (or longest available)
	2.	one strength session (or short mobility if very tight)

How you actually run it via email (super simple workflow)

Step 1: Ask 6 universal questions (works for every athlete)

In every weekly check-in email, collect:
	1.	Main goal right now + any event date?
	2.	What sports did you do last week (and minutes)?
	3.	How many days can you train this week (realistically)?
	4.	Any pain (0–10) + where?
	5.	Energy/stress/sleep (1–10 + quick note)
	6.	Preference: more structure vs more flexibility?

Step 2: Route them to one of 6 “tracks”

Based on Layer 1 + 2, they fall into:
	1.	General Fitness – Low time
	2.	General Fitness – Moderate time
	3.	Main Sport – Base
	4.	Main Sport – Build
	5.	Main Sport – Peak/Taper
	6.	Return-to-training / Risk-managed

Each track has a standard weekly skeleton you fill in.

Step 3: Auto-adjust with the Red/Yellow/Green rule
	•	Red: remove intensity + swap to low impact + reduce volume 20–50%
	•	Yellow: keep volume-ish, reduce intensity (or replace with easy)
	•	Green: proceed

⸻

Example: “Mostly running, swims in winter, 4–6h/week, intermediate”
	•	Track: Main Sport – Base or Build (depends on event)
	•	Weekly skeleton:
	•	Run easy
	•	Run quality (only if Green)
	•	Long run easy
	•	Swim easy recovery OR strength

Example: “Casual, does everything, 2–3h/week”
	•	Track: General Fitness – Low time
	•	Weekly skeleton:
	•	1 easy aerobic (any modality)
	•	1 strength + mobility
	•	1 fun/variety (hike, bike, swim)
