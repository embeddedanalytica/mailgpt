## Running Methodology

## Rules
- Training zones (Daniels framework): easy = conversational aerobic work; threshold/tempo = sustainable hard aerobic work; interval/repetition = shorter, faster work with higher cost
- 80/20 rule: 80% of training time at easy effort, 20% at moderate-to-hard
- The easy run paradox: easy days run too fast make hard days worse; the fix is to slow easy running down, not praise the pace
- Marathon/half-marathon (Pfitzinger): long runs build endurance, medium-long runs maintain it, and tempo/threshold builds race-specific fitness; long runs should not exceed 20-22 miles (marathon) or 14-16 miles (half)
- Key workouts matter more than total mileage — a well-executed tempo produces more race-specific adaptation than junk miles
- Taper (final 2-3 weeks): reduce volume 40-60%, keep some intensity, and do not squeeze in one more hard workout; early-taper sluggishness is normal
- Common injury return patterns: Achilles responds to graduated loading, IT band often needs reduced downhill plus hip strengthening, and shin splints need reduced impact or cross-training
- In injury returns, athlete should be pain-free during easy running before quality sessions
