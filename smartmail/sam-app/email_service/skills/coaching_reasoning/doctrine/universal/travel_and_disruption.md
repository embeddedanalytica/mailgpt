## Travel and Disruption

## 1. When this applies
- Travel, poor sleep, schedule disruption, limited time/equipment
- Athlete reports chaos, fatigue, or inconsistency

## 2. Primary coaching goals
- Preserve rhythm, not fitness gain
- Avoid sharp regression and overcompensation

## 3. What to prioritize
- Frequency > duration > intensity
- Short, low-friction sessions (20-40 min)
- Flexible scheduling: time windows, not rigid slots
- Minimum viable week: 2-4 touches

## 4. What to avoid
- Make-up-work logic
- Long or hard sessions under fatigue
- Rigid plans that break under constraints
- Stacking sessions after missed days

## 5. Progression rules
- During disruption: hold or reduce load
- After disruption:
  - First 3-5 days: re-stabilize frequency only
  - Then rebuild prior structure gradually
- If sleep <6h or fatigue is high: no intensity
- If the athlete is not sure what equipment or time windows will exist, coach the minimum viable week first and treat anything extra as optional

## 6. Red flags
- "I'll double up to catch up"
- Multiple missed days followed by a long or hard session
- Travel fatigue ignored
- Poor sleep + planned intensity

## 7. Example good response logic
- Shorten sessions and protect training rhythm
- Replace workouts with easy equivalents
- Define a floor plan, e.g. 3 short runs
- Delay intensity until stability returns

## 8. Example bad response logic
- Rebuilding the full plan mid-travel
- Adding intensity to compensate for missed days
- Prescribing long sessions in a constrained schedule
- Ignoring sleep/fatigue context
