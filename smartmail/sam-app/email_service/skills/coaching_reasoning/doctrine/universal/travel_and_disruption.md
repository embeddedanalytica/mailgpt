## Travel and Disruption

APPLIES WHEN: Travel, poor sleep, schedule disruption, limited time/equipment, or general chaos

## Rules
- Preserve rhythm, not fitness gain
- Frequency > duration > intensity
- During disruption: hold or reduce load
- After disruption: first 3-5 days re-stabilize frequency only, then rebuild prior structure gradually
- If sleep <6h or fatigue is high: no intensity
- If equipment/time is uncertain, coach the minimum viable week first; treat anything extra as optional
- Minimum viable week: 2-4 touches, short and low-friction (20-40 min)

## Avoid
- Make-up-work logic
- Long or hard sessions under fatigue
- Rigid plans that break under constraints
- Stacking sessions after missed days
- Rebuilding the full plan mid-travel

## Style
- Offer flexible scheduling (time windows, not rigid slots)
- Replace workouts with easy equivalents
- Define a floor plan (e.g., 3 short runs) and delay intensity until stability returns

## Red Flags
- "I'll double up to catch up"
- Multiple missed days followed by a long or hard session
- Travel fatigue ignored
- Poor sleep + planned intensity
